Virat Kohli

The King of Modern Cricket

"Self-belief and hard work will always earn you success." – Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli, also known as "King Kohli," is one of the greatest cricketers in the world. Known for his passion, aggression, and consistency, he has set new benchmarks in modern cricket. From leading India to historic wins to breaking multiple world records, his journey is an inspiration to millions.

Biography Section

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. From a young age, he showed promise as a cricketer. He captained India’s U-19 team to victory in the 2008 World Cup, after which he made his ODI debut for the senior team. Over the years, he evolved into one of the most dependable batsmen in the world, across all formats.

Here's a time line of Virat Kohli Career

  • 2008: Captained India to victory in the U-19 World Cup
  • 2008: Made ODI debut vs Sri Lanka
  • 2011: Won ICC Cricket World Cup
  • 2013: Became India’s vice-captain
  • 2017: Appointed full-time captain across all formats
  • 2018: ICC Cricketer of the Year
  • 2023: Most centuries in ODI history (50+ centuries)
  • 2024: Played a key role in India’s T20 World Cup win
  • 2025: Won IPL title with RCB

Records and Achievements

  • Most centuries in ODIs (50+)
  • Fastest to 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 ODI runs
  • Only player to win ICC Player of the Year in all 3 formats
  • ICC ODI Cricketer of the Decade (2011–2020)
  • Highest T20I run-scorer

Personality & Off-Field Impact

Virat is also known for his fitness revolution in Indian cricket. He's a style icon, a philanthropist, and co-owner of FC Goa (ISL). Along with his wife, actress Anushka Sharma, he supports many causes through the Virat Kohli Foundation.
